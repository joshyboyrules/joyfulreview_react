My Warm Companion: Zojirushi Stainless Mug

Dec 02
Share
0
Article

BRRRRR!! (shaking) It's that time of the year where I'm wishing for some warmer weather. Thankfully I have my16oz Zojirushi Stainless Steel Mugto transfer some warm liquids(mainly water) into my body. There are somepseudo-benefitsto drinking warm water as well but personally I drink warm water cause it warms up my body (I know right).

Keeps Water Warm
If there's something that the Zojirushi mug does well, it is keeping water warm. The mug keeps the water so warm that some people at times will complain about howtoowarm the water stays (which is a dumb reason to complain tbh). I suspect that it accomplishes this through the stellar design of the lid. I can fill up hot liquids at 8am and it will literally stay hot until 8pm. And when you pair this mug with awater boiler, it becomes a winning combo against cold weather.
Design Design Design
As the name suggests, the entire body of the mug is stainless steel, however the lid is made of plastic/rubber material. You can unscrew the lid entirely off the bottle, and the lid itself can be taken apart into four simple parts which make itsuper supereasy to clean (as opposed to thosestrawed Contigo water bottlesthat are difficult to wash). The lid also has a locking mechanism so that it willneverspill in your bag.

Fun Facts: The product dimensions are 2.8 (~7cm) x 2.8 x 8.6 (~22 cm) inches and it weighs 8 ounces (~227 grams). These dimensions/weight are ideal for fitting in any type of cupholder or any backpack-pockets for mugs.

So I should mention that I do agree with the guys from 'Tested' (see Youtube section) that the sip hole is kind of small so it can get annoying to drink out of. So more often than not I find myself unscrewing the entire cap to let the liquids cool and drinking with the cap screwed off. It's minor but I thought it's worth mentioning.

The mug looks sharp. It definitely has a premium look and feel to it; and I guess it should since it retails foraround $27. I've had my Zojirushi for over a year now and I can see minimal scratches/indents/blemishes; it's holding up quite well.

Tip #1 - snap back lid
The lid itself can snap backwards by pushing the lid all the way back which holds the lid in the open position until you snap the lid back forward. I didn't realize this until I accidentally happened; it was a nice surprise, and it's super helpful since the lid won't accidentally hit you in the face when you are pouring hot scorching liquids down your throat

Tip #2 - protect your investment

Be sure to tag/write your name on the mug somewhere just in case you lose it. I put my name and my phone # on it using a piece of paper and some scotch tape. Took 10 minutes, which could save you from buying another one. Also side benefit of letting other people know your phone #, if that's what you're into :P.
